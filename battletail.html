Battle Tail - Level Designer, Gameplay Programmer

In my second year of Falmouth University I worked with a team of 8 peers to produce a unique video game. BattleTail is a four-player couch-combat game about mice brutally fighting for their region and honour! My responsibilities included designing an arena and balancing the combat variables to create a smooth experience. My process involved whiteboxing and set dressing the level in Unity, programming the environment hazards in C#, and tweaking the gameplay variables in combination with play tests with my fellow peers.

  • I started by gathering reference images of Aztec architecture and Mayan temples, which I discussed with the artists.
  • I needed to make the level symmetrical to ensure fairness for four players.
  • I wanted to create an arena with traps, inspired by the association between Aztec temples and Indiana Jones.
  • I programmed the push-trap (see Fig. 3) to break the player's line of movement and push them into the pits at the centre.
  • The geometry at the centre-top and centre-bottom was removed after feedback from playtests (see Fig. 2).
